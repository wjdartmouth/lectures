## SECTION A: READING COMPREHENSION STRATEGY

### The Three Understanding Levels

| Level | What | Answer Starter |
|-------|------|---|
| **RETRIEVAL** | Find info stated directly | "The passage states that..." |
| **INFERENCE** | Read between the lines | "This suggests..." or "We infer..." |
| **INTERPRETATION** | Understand significance | "This reveals..." or "This matters..." |

### The Close Reading Process (5 Steps)

1. **Read once** → No notes; get the whole
2. **Mark unknown words** → Circle unfamiliar words; underline key phrases
3. **Look up meanings** → Use context to guess; then verify
4. **Annotate tone/mood** → Mark shifts in emotion; write questions
5. **Ask big questions** → What is the author saying? Why this detail?

### Answer Formula for Section A

```
QUOTE/REFERENCE + YOUR EXPLANATION + WHY IT MATTERS
```

**❌ Weak:** "Holden is lonely."  
**✅ Strong:** "Holden returns to his 'cosy' room after leaving Spencer, suggesting he seeks isolation and comfort away from adult judgment. This reveals his alienation and need for safe space."

### Common Section A Mistakes

| Mistake | Problem | Fix |
|---------|---------|-----|
| Memory instead of text | Misremembering details | Reread passage for EVERY answer |
| Misquoting | Inaccurate evidence | Quote word-for-word exactly |
| No explanation | Just stating facts | Always explain WHY it matters |
| Mixing retrieval + interpretation | Confusing levels | Keep them separate |

## SECTION B: LITERARY & RHETORICAL TECHNIQUES

### The Technique Sandwich Formula

```
TECHNIQUE NAME → TEXTUAL EVIDENCE → EFFECT ON READER
```

**❌ Weak:** "Salinger uses stream of consciousness."  
**✅ Strong:** "Salinger uses stream of consciousness when Holden digresses from the hat to books to authors (lines 13–24), creating intimate access to his scattered mind and revealing his anxiety and isolation."

### PAPER 1 TECHNIQUES (The Catcher in the Rye)

| Technique | Use | Effect |
|-----------|-----|--------|
| **Stream of consciousness** | Jumpy, digressive thoughts | Intimate; reveals anxious mind |
| **Colloquial language** | "goddam," "corny," "phoney" | Authentic teenage voice |
| **Irony** | Says one thing, means another | Reveals Holden's conflicts |
| **Symbolism** | Red hat, ducks, museum | Layers of meaning |
| **Characterisation** | Direct + Indirect methods | Reveals internal conflict |
| **Tone & mood** | Shifts in emotion | Mirrors Holden's state |

### PAPER 2 TECHNIQUES (The Swerve)

| Technique | Use | Effect |
|-----------|-----|--------|
| **Contrast** | Gold vs. books; classical vs. medieval | Creates memorable argument |
| **Embedded quotation** | Primary sources woven into narrative | Authenticity; immediacy |
| **Narrative non-fiction** | Story + argument; character development | Emotional + intellectual engagement |
| **Paradox** | Christian + pagan; contempt = fame | Reveals complexity |
| **Metaphor** | "Resurrection" for knowledge recovery | Elevates project significance |
| **Tone & voice** | Scholarly yet accessible | Credible; persuasive |

### Common Section B Mistakes

| Mistake | Problem | Fix |
|---------|---------|-----|
| Naming only | No explanation | Add: "This creates..." |
| Technique vs. content | Describing plot, not HOW | Focus on author's choices |
| Missing evidence | No quote | Add specific quote/reference |
| Vague effect | "Creates an effect" | Be specific: sympathise/understand/feel |

## SECTION C: ESSAY WRITING (40 Marks)

### The PEE Paragraph (Your Secret Weapon)

```
POINT (topic sentence) 
    ↓ 
EVIDENCE (quote or scene reference) 
    ↓ 
EXPLANATION (why does it matter?)
```

**Example:**

**POINT:** Holden's stream of consciousness reveals his emotional isolation.

**EVIDENCE:** When describing the hat, Holden jumps from its appearance to authors to books to his feelings (lines 13–24).

**EXPLANATION:** This technique shows his mind works differently from his peers'; his scattered thinking isolates him further. Intelligence doesn't help him connect; it marks him as different.

### Essay Structure

```
INTRODUCTION (2–3 sentences)
├─ Hook or context
├─ Thesis statement (what you'll argue)
└─ Brief roadmap of evidence

BODY PARAGRAPH 1 (4–5 sentences)
├─ Topic sentence (what you're proving)
├─ Evidence (quote or reference)
└─ Explanation (why it matters for your thesis)

BODY PARAGRAPH 2 (4–5 sentences)
├─ Topic sentence
├─ Evidence
└─ Explanation

CONCLUSION (2–3 sentences)
├─ Restate thesis in new words
└─ Final thought on significance
```

### Strong Essay Checklist

- [ ] Clear thesis statement (first paragraph)
- [ ] 3+ pieces of evidence (one strong piece per paragraph minimum)
- [ ] Each evidence integrated into sentence (not dropped in)
- [ ] Explanation for every quote (why does it matter?)
- [ ] Connection to character/theme/meaning
- [ ] Your own words (paraphrase, don't just copy)
- [ ] Formal tone (no texting language)
- [ ] Spell-checked (especially character/author names)

## PAPER 1: THE CATCHER IN THE RYE

### Holden's Character in One Page

**Who:** 16-year-old, recently expelled, intelligent, angry, sarcastic  
**Conflicts:** Wants to stay young but forced to grow up; wants friends but pushes people away  
**Psychology:** Grieving (brother Allie), depressed, obsessed with "phoniness," isolated  
**Voice:** Stream of consciousness, colloquial, vulnerable, sarcastic  
**Key paradox:** Self-aware about his flaws but can't change; judges others harshly while claiming to value authenticity

### The Red Hunting Hat: Quick Analysis

| Aspect | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **What it is** | Red, unusual, conspicuous, childish, worn peak-to-back |
| **Why he bought it** | Impulsively, after losing fencing foils (replacement/comfort) |
| **What he says about it** | "Very corny, I'll admit, but I liked it that way" |
| **What it represents** | Individuality, non-conformity, self-protection, alienation, authenticity |
| **Why it's significant** | Readers remember it more than almost anything; it defines him |

### Key Themes (For Essays)

- **Alienation & isolation** — Holden can't connect; seeks solitude
- **Loss of innocence** — Grieves Allie; fears adulthood
- **Authenticity vs. phoniness** — Obsessed with "real" vs. "fake"
- **Mental health** — Depression, anxiety, breakdown
- **Adolescent contradiction** — Wants to grow up AND stay young

### Common Section A/B Questions

**Comprehension:** What does Holden do when...? What does the passage suggest about...? What tone/mood is established...?

**Technique:** Analyse Salinger's use of [technique]. How does [technique] reveal character? What effect does [technique] create?

**Essay:** How does Salinger present Holden as complex/contradictory? What does the red hat symbolise? How does voice create sympathy and frustration?

## PAPER 2: THE SWERVE

### Greenblatt's Central Argument (One Sentence)

**Poggio's rediscovery of Lucretius's *De rerum natura* sparked the Renaissance by introducing dangerous pagan ideas that reshaped how humans understood themselves and society.**

### Key Players & Ideas

**Petrarch:** Humanist obsessed with classical texts; made recovery a *passion*

**Poggio:** Scribe who found manuscript; obscure figure, enormous consequence

**Lucretius & *De rerum natura*:** Ancient poem; ideas were dangerous to Church

**The "swerve" (clinamen):** Tiny deviation causes collision/change; discovery sparked Renaissance

**Petrarch's paradox:** Christian + pagan obsession = shows worldview gap

### Dangerous Ideas in Lucretius

**Universe:** Atoms in void; no divine design

**Gods:** Exist but don't control world (threatens Church authority)

**Pleasure:** Legitimate good (contradicts Christian asceticism)

**Afterlife:** None; no cosmic purpose (threatens worldview)

**Worldview:** Alternative to religion

### Greenblatt's Rhetorical Moves

| Move | Why It Works | Effect |
|------|---|---|
| **Narrative** | Story, not assertion | Readers care emotionally |
| **Characterisation** | Complex Petrarch | Humanists seem real |
| **Embedded quotation** | Primary sources | Authenticity; immediacy |
| **Contrast** | Gold vs. books | Memorable argument |
| **Authority tone** | Scholarly, confident | Credible; persuasive |
| **Minor figures** | Obscure → significant | Ideas matter; agency matters |

### Key Themes (For Essays)

- **Power of ideas** — Dangerous knowledge shapes history
- **Individual agency** — One person's discovery can matter enormously
- **Fragility of culture** — Books decay; knowledge is lost
- **Classical vs. medieval worldviews** — The "swerve" from one to other
- **Craft & scholarship** — How historian tells story while arguing

### Common Section A/B Questions

**Comprehension:** What does the passage suggest about...? What can we infer from Petrarch/Poggio's behaviour? What is the "swerve"?

**Technique:** Analyse Greenblatt's use of [technique]. How does [technique] build his argument? What effect does [technique] create?

**Essay:** How does Greenblatt build his argument? What is Petrarch's paradox and why does it matter? How does narrative technique make scholarship accessible?
